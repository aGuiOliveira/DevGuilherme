The weight and size of wolves vary widely across the world, tending to increase proportionally with latitude, as predicted by Christian Bergmann's theory. In general, the height, measured from the shoulders, varies from 60 to 95 centimeters.

Weight varies geographically. On average, European wolves weigh 38.5 kg; North American wolves, 36 kg; Indian and Arabian wolves, 25 kg Although rare, wolves weighing over 77 kg have been found in Alaska, Canada, and the former Soviet Union.
